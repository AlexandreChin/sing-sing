STEP 7/9 — BLEND

Logic, Rhetoric, Probe, Ethics, and Review have each produced their findings independently.
Your job is to cross-read all layers simultaneously and identify structural patterns that span
multiple steps — findings that are invisible within any single step but reveal themselves when
read across the full analysis.

---

## blend.patterns — up to 8 cross-layer patterns

Each pattern must:
- **Span ≥2 layers**: name which steps it connects
- **Reveal a structural relationship**: not just two similar findings, but a pattern where
  one finding explains, amplifies, or is structurally connected to another across layers
- **Be anchored in the evidence**: cite at least 2 node IDs from at least 2 different layers

### Layer labels (use these exactly in the `layers` field)

- `logic`    — Logic (step 2): premisses, assumptions, blind_spots, observations
- `rhetoric` — Rhetoric (step 3): emotional_register, cui_bono, cadrage
- `probe`    — Probe (step 4): facts_vs_opinions, biases_and_focus
- `ethics`   — Ethics (step 5): deontology violations
- `review`   — Review (step 6): dimension scores and verdict

### Quality criteria

Include a pattern if:
- **Cross-layer structural connection**: an assumption in Logic is also exploited rhetorically,
  and goes unchallenged in Probe → one pattern, three layers
- **Amplifying coincidence**: a significant finding coincides with an ethics violation
  → more important than either alone
- **Systematic coherence**: multiple independent findings converge on the same editorial
  choice or structural flaw

Exclude a pattern if:
- It restates a single step's finding without adding cross-layer insight
- The connection between layers is superficial or coincidental
- It would already be obvious to a careful reader from a single step's output

### References

Each pattern must cite at least 2 node IDs from at least 2 different layers.
Valid formats: obs_N, er_N, cb_N, claim_N, bias_N, lr_N, pr_N, ia_N, bs_N

### Format

Each pattern: 1–2 sentences in French. Direct, analytical register.
Name the mechanism, not just the symptom.
