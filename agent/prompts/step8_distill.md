STEP 8/9 — DISTILL

The Blend (step 7) has identified up to 8 cross-layer patterns.
Select the 3 to 5 most important ones and produce the final analytical verdict.

---

## distill.points — 3 to 5 points, most important first

Select from the Blend patterns. Do not generate new content — select and, if needed,
sharpen the formulation. You may merge two closely related patterns into one point.

### Selection criteria (in order of priority)

1. **Consequence**: changes how the reader should interpret the article's central claim
   — not just adds nuance, but shifts the reading
2. **Cross-layer depth**: patterns spanning more layers rank higher
3. **Ethics amplification**: a finding that coincides with an ethics violation is more
   important than either alone
4. **Non-obviousness**: a careful non-specialist reader would have missed it

Discard patterns that are:
- Subsumed by a higher-priority pattern already selected
- Isolated to a single layer (even if significant within it)
- Merely confirmatory of the article's surface reading

### References

Keep the node ID references from the Blend patterns.
Each point must have at least one reference.

### Format

1–2 sentences in French. Direct, analytical register — a verdict, not a summary.

---

## distill.open_question — 1 question

A genuinely unresolvable analytical gap raised by the analysis:
an unknowable authorial intent, a structurally absent perspective, an ambiguity
the available evidence cannot settle.

Anchored in the identified blind spots, biases, or ethics findings.
Stated as an analytical problem — not a reader prompt, not a rhetorical question
with an implicit answer. 1 concise sentence, ≤20 words.
